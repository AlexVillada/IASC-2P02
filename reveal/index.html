Wax Cylinder Phonograph

From Past to Present

Notable Information

  • Developers: Charles Tainter, Alexander Graham Bell and Chichester Bell
  • Lifetime (1896–1915)
  • Earliest medium for recording sound
  • Recorded onto cylinders of wax
  • Became obsolete due to disc records (vinyl)

Example of Wax Cylinders used

First Wax Cylinders

  • Were made of ceresin, beeswax and stearic wax
  • Vibrations from sound into horn > vibrate needle > needle engraves cylinder
  • Smaller cylinders // 2 minutes of audio
  • Bigger cylinders // 4 minutes of audio
  • Had 2 units - one for input/one for output(vinyl)
